Cookies and Similar Technologies

Our services employ cookies, web beacons, mobile analytics, advertising IDs, and other similar technologies for operational purposes and data collection, including device identifiers, usage statistics, and device data. Cookies, small data files sent to your computer's hard drive by a website, assist in enhancing your online experience. We use both session cookies, which expire when you close your browser, and persistent cookies, which remain on your computer until removed, to offer a tailored and engaging experience on our website. Cookies typically contain a unique alphanumeric identifier for your device, but may include additional data as well.

Our websites feature web beacons (also known as single-pixel gifs), electronic images that aid in cookie delivery, count website visitors, and compile performance data. We incorporate web beacons in our promotional emails and newsletters to track if they are opened and interacted with.

Mobile analytics and advertising IDs, created by mobile operating systems like iOS and Android, are utilized by apps in a similar manner to how websites use cookies. Our applications include software allowing third-party analytics and advertising partners to access these mobile IDs.

We and our third-party partners use these technologies to gather personal information (such as browser cookies, mobile device advertising IDs, and your device's IP address) when you use our Services. This technology also helps us and our partners collect data about your online activities across various websites over time. The information gathered is used for multiple purposes: to remember your preferences and settings, facilitate sign-in, provide targeted advertising, prevent fraud, analyze service performance, track your interactions with our Services, develop insights, and other legitimate functions. This collected or inferred information is also shared with third parties for similar purposes.

California “Do Not Sell My Info”

Under the California Consumer Protection Act (“CCPA”), Alternative Medicine News is required to inform you about the categories of personal data that are sold to third parties and provide guidance on opting out of future sales. According to the CCPA, personal information encompasses online identifiers, which include IP addresses, cookie IDs, and mobile IDs. The law interprets a “sale” as making data available to third parties in some instances. At Alternative Medicine News, we allow advertising and analytics providers to collect your IP addresses, cookie IDs, and mobile IDs via our websites and apps when you utilize our online services, but we do not engage in the “sale” of any other types of personal information. If you prefer that Alternative Medicine News and our partners do not “sell” your personal data for advertising purposes, you can submit a Do Not Sell Request by emailing alternativemedicineweekly@gmail.com Please note, even after we receive your request, we might still share some personal information with our partners (acting as service providers) to assist with advertising tasks such as measuring ad effectiveness, controlling ad frequency, reporting on ad performance, ensuring service functionality and security, providing aggregated statistics and analytics, optimizing ad placement, and reducing ad fraud. Emailing us does not automatically opt you out of the utilization of previously sold personal information or halt all interest-based advertising. Additionally, if you use other devices or browsers, you should visit the link provided below through those devices or browsers to ensure your choices are applied to the data collected through them. Further details about your rights to opt out of personal data sales are included in the California Privacy Rights section of this Notice.

Cookie Management

Typically, web browsers accept cookies by default. You can alter your browser settings to delete or reject cookies. However, doing so might impact certain website features or services. Deleting cookies may also remove settings and preferences governed by those cookies, including advertising preferences, which may need to be reset.

Mobile Advertising ID Management

The operating systems for iOS and Android offer options to limit tracking and reset advertising IDs.

Email Web Beacons

You can modify your email client settings to prevent the automatic downloading of images, disabling web beacons in the emails you read.

Do Not Track Settings

Some browsers feature “Do Not Track” (DNT) settings that send a signal to websites indicating your preference not to be tracked. However, as there is no universally accepted method for interpreting DNT signals, our websites currently do not respond to browser DNT signals. You can instead use various tools for controlling data collection and use, including the cookie and advertising controls mentioned above.
